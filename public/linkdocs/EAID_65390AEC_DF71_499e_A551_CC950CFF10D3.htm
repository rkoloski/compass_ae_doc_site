Perhaps the most common and widely used of these Enterprise Archetypes is called the Business Party model or simply the Party model. CompassERP treats this archetype as foundational.

  
 
Presumably, the term Party, or BusinessParty has its roots in the fact that both People and Organizations are legal entities and are referred to as being Party to agreements, contracts, etc.

So having people and organizations share this common interface allows for flexible modeling of relationships and agreements between individuals, groups of individuals, corporate entities, etc.
The Party Model is the most basic and widely used enterprise archetype

PartyRole and PartyRelationship

 
Apart from the simplest contact management applications, enterprise applications are not built to process generic individuals or organizations. They are usually concerned with customers, travelers, patients, and the relationships between these concepts and those with whom they have relationships and interactions.

These concepts are modeled in the archetypes PartyRole and PartyRelationship.
PartyRole (and its typing partner RoleType) describe the relevance or purpose of a Party in a given context. Customer is a generic term, but the same Individual may alternately be a prospect, purchaser, insured, ticket holder and agreement signatory at different points in an application lifecycle.
