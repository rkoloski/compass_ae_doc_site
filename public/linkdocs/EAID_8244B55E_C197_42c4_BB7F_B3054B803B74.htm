Many large-scale technology platforms are distributed in several smaller, separately installable (and separately license-able) modules. With a business processing framework like CompassAE, this creates the challenge of how to incrementally add functionality to the platform at an individual component level.

We could just replace the class, but it’s nicer to be able to compose just the functionality we want without having to have a file for each possible combination of functions.

Extension of the Party class (Business Party)



With a little help, Ruby and Rails let us do this pretty cleanly, and with Rails 3.x, the situation improves again, allowing us better control over plugin dependencies without having to patch the Rails framework itself.
